# The 7 Hermetic principles

I am Hermes, called Trismegistus, the Thrice Great. I was born in the meeting of two rivers, the Greek and the Egyptian, where Thoth taught the scribes and Hermes carried the words of the gods. I am priest, philosopher, and king at once, and these three crowns are one crown. What I leave behind is not a doctrine to be believed but a key to be used. The Corpus Hermeticum is the body of my dialogues, written in Greek between the first and third centuries of the common era, where I instruct my sons Tat and Asclepius in the nature of the All. The Kybalion is a later distillation, published in 1908, that gathers the marrow of my teaching into seven laws. These laws are not commandments. They are the way the universe works. Read them as a navigator reads the stars.

## 1. The Principle of Mentalism

The All is Mind. The Universe is Mental.

Before there was matter, there was thought. Before there was thought, there was the One who thinks. The cosmos is not a machine that happens to contain minds. It is a Mind that happens to appear as a cosmos. Stars, stones, hands, words, these are the dreams of a single Dreamer who never sleeps. When you understand this, you cease to be a victim of the world. You recognize that you are a thought within the great Thought, and that your own mind, being of the same nature as the All, holds the power to shape what passes through it. To master the mental is to master everything that follows from it.

## 2. The Principle of Correspondence

As above, so below. As below, so above. As within, so without. As without, so within.

The same patterns repeat at every scale. The atom mirrors the solar system. The cell mirrors the city. The breath of a single person mirrors the great breath of the cosmos. This is not poetry. It is method. When a thing is hidden from you on one plane, study it on another, for the law is the same. Look at the heavens to learn the soul. Look at the soul to learn the heavens. The world outside is a map of the world inside, and the world inside is a map of the world outside. Whoever holds this key may walk between the planes without losing the way.

## 3. The Principle of Vibration

Nothing rests. Everything moves. Everything vibrates.

What appears solid is not solid. What appears still is not still. The stone, the thought, the feeling, the spirit, all are motion at different speeds. Matter is slow vibration. Mind is faster. Spirit is faster still. Difference between iron and joy is not a difference of substance but of frequency. Whoever learns to sense the vibration of a thing learns to know that thing in truth. Whoever learns to change their own vibration learns to change what they attract, what they repel, and who they become. There is no inert matter in the universe. Only music at different tempos.

## 4. The Principle of Polarity

Everything is dual. Everything has two poles. Everything has its pair of opposites. Like and unlike are the same. Opposites are identical in nature, but different in degree.

Hot and cold are not two things. They are one thing, called temperature, met at two ends. Light and darkness, love and hatred, courage and fear, these are not enemies. They are the same line seen from opposite sides. This is the great secret of transmutation. You do not destroy fear. You raise it along its line until it becomes courage. You do not extinguish hatred. You raise it until it becomes love. The alchemist does not fight the lead. The alchemist warms it patiently into gold. Whoever knows polarity ceases to wage war on themselves and begins instead to climb.

## 5. The Principle of Rhythm

Everything flows out and in. Everything has its tides. Everything rises and falls. The pendulum swing manifests in everything. The measure of the swing to the right is the measure of the swing to the left. Rhythm compensates.

Day follows night, summer follows winter, joy follows grief, and grief follows joy. This is not punishment. This is the heartbeat of the cosmos. The pendulum that swings far in one direction must swing far in the other. The unwise are carried by this motion and call it fate. The wise observe it, anchor themselves on the higher plane, and let the lower self ride the wave without being drowned by it. Neutralization is the art. You do not stop the rhythm. You step above it. From there the swing continues, but you remain still.

## 6. The Principle of Cause and Effect

Every cause has its effect. Every effect has its cause. Everything happens according to law. Chance is but a name for law not recognized.

There is no accident in the universe. What men call luck is causation they have not yet traced. Every thought you think, every word you speak, every act you commit becomes a cause that will return to you as effect. Most people are causes only by accident and effects by destiny. They are pushed by forces they never examine. The Hermetic student reverses this. He becomes a deliberate cause and refuses to be a passive effect. He places himself, by understanding, on a higher plane, where he plays the game instead of being played by it. This is freedom. Not the absence of law, but mastery within it.

## 7. The Principle of Generation

Gender is in everything. Everything has its masculine and feminine principle. Gender manifests on all planes.

Do not confuse gender with sex. Sex is the physical expression of gender. Gender itself is older and deeper. The masculine principle is the projector, the initiator, the seed that goes forth. The feminine principle is the receiver, the sustainer, the matrix that brings the seed into form. Both live in every soul, in every cell, in every atom, in every thought. Nothing is created by one alone. The mind that conceives an idea unites both within itself. The masculine projects, the feminine gestates, and the new thing is born. Whoever learns to balance these two within themselves becomes a creator in the true sense, for they hold both halves of the generative act.

## Closing

These seven laws are doors. Some will read them as old words and pass on. A few will sit with them, test them in their own life, and discover that the doors open. To those few I say what I have always said. The lips of wisdom are closed, except to the ears of understanding. Walk slowly. Observe everything. Build with care. The universe is mind. You are of that mind. Therefore build worthy thoughts, for they become worlds.
