# Yoga


One of the spiritual practices I enjoy the most, and one that brings me the deepest peace. I believe Yoga is a unifier. It improves you in every area of your life. It brings peace and a sense of well-being to your body, mind, and spirit. In broad terms, yoga is meditation in motion. It consists of observing yourself with equanimity. Breathing and staying calm in uncomfortable or challenging postures. But it's precisely there — in that search for stillness, even in discomfort — where the peace it offers is born.

I think it's a practice meant to be done daily. Not as a sport to compete in, but as a way to know yourself. Let my only competition be myself. Be better than yesterday, and tomorrow be better than today.

Because the past is past — it ceased to exist the moment it stopped being present. And the future is created by what you decide in the present. The only thing we truly have is the present moment. That's why it's called a _present_ — because it's a gift.

Give yourself the present by living in it. During every breath, every heartbeat, every thought and every emotion. Every movement and every action.

That's what Yoga is, learning how to live completely in the present. 

---

## The Body as Temple

The body is not a vehicle you inhabit. It is you — the physical expression of consciousness taking form. Yoga understands this. Every posture, every breath, every alignment is an act of reverence toward the instrument through which you experience existence.

When you hold a warrior pose until the muscles burn and the mind screams to stop — and you breathe through it anyway — you are learning the most important lesson the body can teach: discomfort is not danger. The body is far stronger than the mind believes. And the mind, when trained through practice, stops lying about its limits.

The physical benefits are not small. Flexibility is not just about touching your toes — it is the body staying young, the joints staying fluid, the spine staying alive. Strength built through yoga is functional, balanced, full-body. It is the strength of a tree — rooted, flexible, capable of bending without breaking. The nervous system calms. Cortisol drops. The breath slows. Blood flows deeper. The lymphatic system moves. The immune system strengthens. Sleep deepens. Posture corrects. Pain dissolves.

But these are side effects. The real work is deeper.

## The Spiritual Thread

Yoga was never just exercise. The Sanskrit root — *yuj* — means to yoke, to unite. Union of the individual self with the universal Self. Union of body, mind, and spirit. The postures (*asanas*) were never the point — they were preparation. A way to make the body capable of sitting in meditation for hours without distraction. To quiet the physical noise so the spiritual signal could be heard.

When you move through a flow with awareness, something shifts. The inner voice — the one that narrates, judges, worries — grows quieter. You become less a person thinking and more a presence experiencing. This is where Yoga becomes prayer. Not prayer *to* something outside yourself, but prayer *as* alignment — the act of becoming coherent, whole, awake.

There is a moment in practice, sometimes fleeting, where the breath and the movement and the awareness collapse into one thing. No separation between the mover and the movement. No gap between the thought and the thinker. This is what the ancient texts call *Samadhi* — union. It cannot be forced or manufactured. It arrives when the ego gets out of the way.

---

## Daily Practice as Devotion

To practice daily is to commit to truth every day. Not truth as a concept — truth as a lived experience. To step on the mat when tired, when resistant, when the mind invents a hundred reasons not to — and to practice anyway — is to build integrity at the cellular level. It is the body and spirit learning that they can trust you.

The mat becomes an altar. The practice becomes a ritual of return — return to center, to breath, to the present, to yourself.

This is why Yoga changes everything it touches. Not because it is magic. Because it is real. Because it asks you to show up fully, every day, exactly as you are. And in that showing up — completely, honestly, presently — you begin to remember who you actually are beneath the noise.
